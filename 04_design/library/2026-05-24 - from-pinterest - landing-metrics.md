# Landing Page Metrics

**Product:** From Pinterest
**What:** Landing Page Metrics Section
**Captured:** 2026-05-24

## Screenshot
![[Screenshot 2026-05-24 at 14.35.58.png]]
## What's the design decision

- Dark bg, rectangular cards horizontally aligned. A tag with a status circle and a subheading figure at the top. Low opacity borders with white corners made prominent for the cards.

## Why does it work

- I believe that it works because nowadays in landing page UI, especially made by AI, cards tend to have rounded corners so this makes the section stand out. The content explaining the cards' illustrations are well separated which allows the user to understand what he is looking at. The minimalism in text content allows quick skimming and the user just needs to look at the title and the illustrations to understand straight away what the section is about.

## Underlying principle

- Here the underlying principles are alignement (horizontal), contrast (betweent the image illustration and its description), and colour (vibrant colours used for the illustration vs dark colours for the actual section and probably the full landing page.

## Where else have I seen this pattern

- More and more web apps are using straight corners instead of rounded such as Hermes Agent which I have recently used for my personal use case. Their landing page heavily relies on straight, un-rounded borders to display information.

## What I'd change (if anything)

- I like the design of the cards however I feel like the actual illustrations that are being displayed INSIDE the card themselves (not the background which i am fond of) feel low-quality and don't actually convey as well as they should what the section is about.
